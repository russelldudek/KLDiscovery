Learn the matter. Instrument the truth. Ship one closed loop.

90-day outcome

A bounded, operator-approved pilot that connects one recurring workflow, one meaningful anomaly family, one explicit human-authority path, one client-safe status translation, and one baseline scorecard-without disrupting live matter delivery.

Entry principles

Work before software

Observe the real matter lifecycle, exceptions, language, and judgment before prescribing platform changes.

Value before consolidation

Choose integration and application decisions based on measurable workflow value, not architectural neatness alone.

AI inside authority

Specify what AI may observe, recommend, draft, route, or execute-and where human approval is mandatory.

Client truth from operating truth

Design portal visibility from trusted events and decisions, not a parallel reporting burden.

Days 1-30 · Read the operating system

Objectives

Build domain fluency, relationships, a current-state matter map, and an evidence-based opportunity shortlist.

Listen and shadow

  • Shadow representative matters across intake, forensics, collection, processing, review, production, billing, and client communication.
  • Interview Client Services, Operations, Product, Engineering, the AI Architect, Quality, Security, Finance, and frontline users.
  • Learn the operating language: matter archetypes, SLAs, exception classes, defensibility controls, privilege boundaries, and client decision points.

Map and baseline

  • Map systems, APIs, data stores, event sources, manual handoffs, queues, workarounds, and reporting steps.
  • Document current metric definitions and where they conflict or lack event evidence.
  • Identify the ten most frequent or costly exception families, then validate with operators and data.

Choose one consequential workflow and design the full product loop.

Objectives

Select a pilot where orchestration, anomaly detection, human authority, client visibility, and measurement can be tested together.

Pilot selection gates

Recurring

The friction appears often enough to justify a reusable pattern.

Observable

Expected and actual state can be measured from reliable events or bounded instrumentation.

Consequential

Improvement matters to cycle time, quality, SLA, client effort, or operating cost.

Governable

Authority, exceptions, and release decisions can be made explicit.

Integrable

A pilot can use existing systems without requiring a full platform rewrite.

Adoptable

Frontline teams can understand the benefit and shape the workflow.

Co-design the contract

  • Define the pilot's expected states, transitions, evidence, owner, SLA, authority boundary, and client-safe status language.
  • Specify integration events and the minimum instrumentation needed to distinguish delay, defect, missing context, and legitimate variation.
  • Partner with the AI Architect to select the smallest appropriate detection method: deterministic rules, statistical thresholds, classification, retrieval-assisted reasoning, or agent workflow.
  • Define evaluation before launch: precision/recall where relevant, false-negative cost, intervention burden, detection lead time, operational outcome, and rollback criteria.
  • Create a prototype PRD that reads like a blueprint: user, workflow, states, events, exception paths, controls, telemetry, acceptance tests, and client translation.

SHIP ONE CLOSED LOOP · DECIDE WHAT SCALES

Prove the mechanism in live work-carefully.

Objectives

Launch a controlled pilot, monitor it with operators, close exceptions, compare outcomes, and turn learning into a portfolio decision.

Pilot execution

Before release

  • Run scenario, edge-case, failure-mode, access, security, data-lineage, and rollback reviews.
  • Confirm named owners, support path, escalation timing, client communication rules, and manual fallback.
  • Train users on the new decision model, not only the interface.

During release

  • Use a bounded cohort and daily operator review during the initial period.
  • Capture false positives, missed exceptions, workarounds, trust signals, and adoption friction.
  • Keep one visible decision log connecting signal, action, authority, outcome, and product learning.

After release

  • Compare cycle time, quality, SLA, automation coverage, first-touch resolution, and client/service effort against the documented baseline.
  • Review model and workflow evidence with Product, Ops, CS, Engineering, Quality, and executive sponsors.
  • Decide: scale, revise, integrate, standardize, hold, or stop.

Portfolio output

  • Prioritized roadmap across the three flagship products.
  • Reusable Matter State Contract schema and event vocabulary.
  • Anomaly evaluation and human-authority pattern.
  • Portal translation pattern and measurement scorecard.

Day-90 executive readout

What changed

Workflow, exception, decision, client visibility, and measured outcome.

What was learned

Data quality, model behavior, operational variation, adoption, and economics.

What happens next

Scale decision, technical debt, ownership, investment, and next pilot.

Success evidence by day 90

  • A trusted relationship map and clear decision-right model across Product, CS, Ops, Engineering, AI, Quality, Security, and Finance.
  • A validated current-state opportunity map tied to event evidence and business outcomes.
  • One production-adjacent or production pilot completed under KLDiscovery's release standards, with no invented promise of immediate enterprise transformation.
  • A roadmap that links each investment to cycle time, quality, SLA, automation, first-touch resolution, client trust, or reusable platform capability.
