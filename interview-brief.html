Make matter state executable.

Candidate thesis

KLDiscovery can create disproportionate leverage by defining one operational truth across workflow orchestration, anomaly detection, and client visibility. A shared Matter State Contract makes every handoff machine-actionable, every exception accountable, and every client update traceable to the work itself.

What the role is really being hired to accomplish

Orchestrate

Replace manual handoffs and application fragmentation with explicit states, integration events, ownership, and safe automation from intake through production.

Assure

Surface quality and SLA divergence early, connect AI detection to evidence and human authority, and reduce defects before client exposure.

Reveal

Give clients real-time, comprehensible visibility into status, lineage, SLA, billing, and choices without exposing raw operational noise.

The company moment

KLDiscovery publicly positions itself around AI-driven eDiscovery tools, disciplined execution, deep domain expertise, integrated global teams, and technology-enabled client services. Its current product direction includes generative AI inside Nebula, expanding client visibility, and strengthened executive product leadership. The Director role is therefore a bridge between product ambition and the operating system that must make that ambition trustworthy at scale.

Five operating tensions

Speed vs defensibility

Automation must shorten time without weakening lineage, evidence, quality controls, or accountable release.

Standardization vs matter judgment

Common patterns must support legitimate case variation rather than erase it.

AI detection vs human accountability

Models can surface divergence; named people must still own consequential decisions.

Internal truth vs client-safe truth

The portal must translate real operating state, not reconstruct reassuring status after the fact.

Operating sequence

1. Define

Specify expected state, evidence, owner, SLA, authority, and client-safe translation for a bounded matter archetype.

2. Observe

Instrument event sources across systems and teams; make missing, conflicting, or late state visible.

3. Compare

Use rules, statistical signals, and AI where appropriate to identify meaningful divergence.

4. Decide

Route the exception to the correct human authority with evidence, options, and consequences.

5. Publish

Translate the resolved operating state into useful client status, SLA, lineage, billing, or self-service action.

6. Learn

Retain cause, action, outcome, threshold performance, recurrence, and product decision.

Product architecture implications

  • Common state and event vocabulary: the three flagship products cannot compound if each describes the matter differently.
  • Exception-first product design: straight-through automation is valuable, but trust is won when divergence is detected, explained, owned, and closed.
  • AI evaluation tied to work: precision, recall, calibration, false-negative cost, intervention rate, and business outcome must be reviewed by exception family.
  • Portal translation layer: client visibility should be derived from operational events with disclosure rules-not a separate manual reporting workflow.

Operational excellence is a connected measurement system.

Flow

Cycle time: elapsed and active time by stage, handoff, exception, and matter archetype.
First-touch resolution: work resolved without reassignment, missing context, or duplicate intake.

Assurance

Quality rate: first-pass acceptance, escaped defects, repeated variance, and evidence completeness.
Detection performance: useful warning lead time, false positives, false negatives, and intervention rate.

Commitment

On-time SLA: forecast accuracy, milestone attainment, recovery time, and communication latency.
Client trust: portal engagement, self-service completion, escalation reason, and status clarity.

Leverage

Automation coverage: eligible work automated, safe straight-through completion, and exception cause.
Reuse: percentage of new workflows assembled from established state, integration, evaluation, and portal patterns.

Stakeholder compact

Product owns the coherent outcome and roadmap. Operations and Client Services own work reality, matter judgment, and adoption. Engineering and the AI Architect own safe, supportable implementation and evaluation. Quality, Security, Legal, and Finance define non-negotiable controls and economic truth. Clients validate whether visibility reduces effort and increases trust.

Immediate contribution and domain ramp

Russell does not substitute adjacent operating experience for eDiscovery expertise. He contributes immediately in workflow productization, high-consequence quality systems, AI-enabled process design, technical translation, instrumentation, customer-backward operations, and cross-functional execution-while learning matter mechanics directly through shadowing, operator interviews, product architecture review, and explicit decision-right mapping.
